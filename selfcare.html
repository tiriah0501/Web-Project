Self Care

While working from home, it is important to remember to take care of ourselves.

“Self-care is your fuel...Whatever the road ahead or the path you’ve taken, self-care is what keeps your motor running and your wheels turning.”― Melissa Steginus.

Home Tech Hire

Working on Yourself. It’ll blow your mind.

Working from home all day could have an adverse effect on your mental health. Where, the stress of the workload or having to self-isolate due to the pandemics may have taken a toll on your mental wellbeing. Here at TK Tech hire we have created some tips on how to work on yourself through these times to ensure your productivity levels and mental health is maintained.

Tip 1: Create a playlist to help keep your motivation level ups!

Depending on what genre of music you listen to, creating a personalised playlist of your best hits can help increase your productivity levels to allow you to focus more on your tasks. Listening to music that you enjoy can also have the effect of making the day feel like it is going quicker and your tasks seeming less daunting.

Tip 2: Find a new hobby or develop an existing one to do in your spare time!

Whether it be reading a book, brushing up on your tech skills, cooking, shopping, or anything you have an interest in. Doing something in your spare time either in breaks or after you finish work, will allow you to break away from work mode whilst also developing a new skill or perfecting an existing one.

Let’s get physical. See for yourself.

Sitting down all day for longer periods of time can not only affect your posture but also your energy and concentration levels. Working from home may not offer the same practicalities an office does, as well as it potentially causing you to develop habits that have a negative impact on your physical well-being including not getting enough sleep. Nevertheless, do not stress we have got you!

Tip 1: Get Active!

Whether it is going for a run before or after your work schedule, walking instead of driving to the shop on your lunch break or utilising the objects in your home in replacement of a gym, finding new ways in your home to get active can help with breaking bad habits developed from working from home. Adding a form of exercise in your routine can your body to have breaks from sitting down all day and clearing your mind before carrying out your next task.

Tip 2: Meditation!

Meditation is a practice where an individual uses techniques – such as mindfulness, or focusing the mind on a particular object, thought, or activity – to train attention and awareness, and achieve a mentally clear and emotionally calm and stable state. Implementing meditation in your daily routine can help alleviate the stress and/or anxiety brought upon by a heavy workload or repetitive tasks through releasing the tension from your mind and body. It can also help you achieve an improved and regular sleep schedule/ pattern.

Recommendation 1: Talk to someone!!

If you are struggling with any area of your life whether it be work or personal, do not be afraid to reach out and speak to somebody about this. You can access support networks available to you through your GP, friends, family and so on. You could also initiate this conversation by reaching out to somebody over Zoom/Teams/Skype etc given the current circumstances and the allocated restrictions in your area. You also create your own support bubble where you all check on each other’s mental and physical health.

Recommendation 2: Create a to-do list!

Organising your day is key to ensuring you get all your tasks done within their deadlines. Therefore, we recommend creating a to-do list of all the things you need to get done in the day, whilst also ensuring you factor in time for meditations, being active, and listening to your playlist to in term achieve a better mental and physical well-being. To make this even easier for you there is loads of apps on both iOS and Android that have can give you built in access to to-do list on your phones, laptops, tablets, desktops that can send you daily reminders to make sure you are on track.
